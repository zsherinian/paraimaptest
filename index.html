About the Project

The PARAI Mapping Project is a research effort focused on visualizing and analyzing geographic data through interactive mapping tools. Our goal is to make spatial information easier to explore, understand, and act on.

This page provides a brief overview of the project and a direct link to the current working prototype. The project is actively in development, and this site will be updated as new features and findings become available.

Community Engagement

Community engagement and collaboration are central to the project. Our collaborative research takes three forms.

First, our team brings together scholars, cultural activists, arts-business and media specialists, diasporic Tamil community members, and hereditary parai artists.

Second, we involve drummers directly in data collection — both across Tamil Nadu and in diasporic Tamil communities such as Mauritius, South Africa, and Malaysia. This includes training younger drummers in recording technology to document their hereditary practice, interview their elders, and build the leadership and business skills needed to promote themselves and their groups.

Third, we engage the wider Tamil diaspora to test the map and to build support, engagement, and empathy for the musical sophistication of hereditary artists. We especially want to include North American parai players to help us collect survey data from hereditary drummers in your ancestral villages during visits to India — connecting your practice here to its living roots.

Our goal is to help build stronger, mutually beneficial relationships between diasporic Tamils and hereditary parai artists.
